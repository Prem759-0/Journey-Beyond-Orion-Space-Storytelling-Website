MISSION: DEEP SPACE — ORION SECTOR

Journey BEYOND ORION

An interstellar voyage 2,400 light years from Earth. Humanity's boldest mission — six astronauts, one ship, infinite possibility.

Chapter 01 — Departure

Earth's Last
Sunrise

On March 14, 2041, the Orion Pathfinder broke atmosphere at Mach 12. The crew watched our blue marble shrink to a marble, then a star, then nothing. There is a peculiar silence when the last photon of home reaches your eye.

Chapter 02 — The Crossing

Through the
Asteroid Belt

40,000 rocks. Some the size of cities. The AI navigation ran 11 million collision predictions per second. We threaded the needle at 0.8% the speed of light, close enough to feel the gravel against the hull.

Chapter 03 — The Giants

The Gas
Giant Field

We used Jupiter's gravity well to slingshot 18% closer to our target. As we swung past, we spent six hours in the shadow of its storms — each tempest larger than Earth. None of us spoke.

Chapter 04 — The Destination

Betelgeuse
Proximity

We arrived at the observation boundary of Betelgeuse — a red supergiant 700 times wider than our sun. It fills half the sky. Every instrument is recording. Every crew member is weeping. This is why we came.
